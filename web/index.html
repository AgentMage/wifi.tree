why it matters

Off-grid, not out of touch

There's a particular knot in your stomach when you roll past the last bar of signal. The trip's supposed to set you free — but somewhere back home, someone's wondering if you made it, and you've got no way to tell them.

wifi.mom unties that knot. Not with a firehose of internet — just enough to send "I'm here, I'm safe, I love you" and feel the worry let go on both ends. Then the phone goes back in the pack and the woods are yours again.

wood-speed/wʊd spiːd/ adjective · internet

Deliberately slow internet, shared in places that have none. Fast enough to find your friends, message home, and check the map — gentle enough to stay fair when a whole camp is on one dish. Sips battery. Leaves the woods wild.

It's wood-speed out here, family — load the map and go hug a tree.

how it works

One dish. A whole gathering online.

From the rainbow parking lot to the back of the woods.

01
🛰️
You bring the signal

Starlink, a cell hotspot, whatever you hauled into the wild. One connection, somewhere it has no business existing.

⋮
02
🌳
You plant a tree

A tiny device — a wifi.tree/sprout (ESP32) or wifi.tree/sapling (Raspberry Pi Zero) — quietly shares your uplink as wood-speed wifi across camp. Sips power, plays nice with solar.

⋮
03
🌿
The camp grows leaves

Neighbors connect, type a name, and grow a leaf to get online. Just enough signal to find their people and say they made it. Leaves fall after a few hours — grow another whenever.

⋮
04
🌈
It spreads to the next gathering

Someone loves it enough to bring their own next time. It's open source, so anyone can. One wifi.mom becomes a forest of them.
