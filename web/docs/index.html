Become a wifi.mom

share your signal with the whole camp

Got a Starlink dish or a hotspot and a patch of wilderness full of people with no signal? You've got everything you need to be a wifi.mom. Pick a tree, plant it, and your uplink becomes wood-speed wifi for the gathering. The product is the easy path — but the source is open if you'd rather build your own for personal or community use.

the easy way

wifi.tree/sprout

ESP32 · ~$10 · simple

A chip the size of a stick of gum that does the whole job on its own — wifi, DNS, and the portal, all on board. Perfect for the parking lot, a camp, a pop-up, or anywhere you want the least possible gear to haul.

Order one ready to go → Or flash an ESP32 yourself →

wifi.tree/sapling

Raspberry Pi Zero · ~$35+ · more visitors

More room to grow: data quotas, fancier traffic shaping, and a full visitor history. Best for a base camp or a long gathering where you want the extra knobs. For those who have Raspberry Pi Zero W 1 or 2 and dont want to buy or build a sprout.

before you roll out

What you'll need

🛰️
A signal to share

Starlink, a cell hotspot, any uplink you packed in. Your tree connects to it and quietly re-shares it as wood-speed wifi across camp.

🪴
A tree to plant

A wifi.tree/sprout (any common ESP32, ~$10 — or order one ready to go) or a wifi.tree/sapling (Raspberry Pi Zero W 1 or 2).

🔋
A little power

A USB battery, a solar bank, or a cigarette-lighter adapter. A sprout sips so little you'll forget it's on. Flash it once at home, then it runs itself.

the gentle philosophy

Why a tree, and why wood-speed?

A wifi.tree is deliberately slow. The default cap is 100 kbps per visitor — plenty for messaging, maps, and saying you made it in, but not enough for anyone to swallow the whole dish. One uplink, a whole camp, kept fair. That's wood-speed.

Leaves expire after a few hours. It keeps the guest list fresh and gently reminds everyone the signal is shared and alive. Nobody's ever locked out — you can always grow a new leaf.

And it's all yours to shape: the tree's name, the emoji, the welcome note, the accent color, the leaf lifetime, the speed cap. Make it sound like your camp. It's your tree, your gathering, your rules.
